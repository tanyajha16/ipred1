Home page content Rice (Oryza sativa) is a staple food crop for over half of the world population and its productivity is largely affected by blast disease caused by the fungus Magnaporthe grisea. The rice blast fungus starts its infection cycle when a three-celled conidium lands on the rice leaf surface. Annual yield losses of rice destroyed by blast fungus are sufficient to feed 60 million people. In the past decade, many biochemical techniques have been developed to verify PPIs, the experimental identification of PPIs between plant and pathogen is still a time-consuming and challenging work. Thus, computational method is an effective alternative for identification of PPI s between rice and blast fungus. The computational identified PPI will be very helpful in guiding the experimental methods. RM-in-Pred is an attempt in this direction which is a Support Vector Machine (SVM) based tool for identifying as well as classifying the rice and Magnaporthe grisea protein pair. Varity of features Amino acid composition, and conjoint triad were exploited in the development of this prediction method.
